About kathmandu

Kathmandu

[a] officially Kathmandu Metropolitan City,[b] is the capital and most populous city of Nepal with 845,767 inhabitants living in 105,649 households as of the 2021 Nepal census[4] and 2.9 million people in its urban agglomeration. It is located in the Kathmandu Valley, a large valley in the high plateaus in central Nepal, at an altitude of 1,400 metres (4,600 feet). The city is one of the oldest continuously inhabited places in the world, founded in the 2nd century AD.

The valley was historically called the "Nepal Mandala" and has been the home of the Newar people, a cosmopolitan urban civilization in the Himalayan foothills. The city was the royal capital of the Kingdom of Nepal and hosts palaces, mansions and gardens built by the Nepali aristocracy. It has been home to the headquarters of the South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC) since 1985. Today, it is the seat of government of the Federal Democratic Republic of Nepal, established in 2008, and is part of Bagmati Province.

Specialties of Kathmandu City

Historical Significance
Kathmandu is steeped in history, with a legacy that dates back thousands of years. The city has served as the center of power for various dynasties and kingdoms throughout history.
World Heritage Sites
Kathmandu is home to seven UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including Durbar Squares, Swayambhunath Stupa, Boudhanath Stupa, Pashupatinath Temple, and Changunarayan Temple.
Cultural Diversity
The city boasts a vibrant mix of cultures and traditions, with influences from Hinduism, Buddhism, and other ethnic groups. Visitors can experience a rich tapestry of festivals, rituals, and ceremonies throughout the year.
Traditional Newari Architecture
Kathmandu is renowned for its traditional Newari architecture, characterized by intricately carved wooden windows, doors, and temples. The city's historic quarters are adorned with beautifully crafted buildings that reflect the artistic prowess of the Newar artisans.
Adventure Tourism
Kathmandu serves as the main gateway to the Himalayas, attracting trekkers and mountaineers from around the world. The city is a bustling hub for adventure tourism, offering access to iconic trekking routes, including the Everest Base Camp Trek and the Annapurna Circuit.
Thamel
Thamel is Kathmandu's bustling tourist district, known for its vibrant street life, eclectic shops, and bustling nightlife. Visitors can explore a wide range of restaurants, bars, handicraft shops, and budget accommodations in this lively neighborhood.
Spiritual Retreats
With its numerous temples, monasteries, and meditation centers, Kathmandu offers ample opportunities for spiritual exploration and retreat. Visitors can participate in yoga sessions, meditation retreats, and spiritual workshops amidst serene surroundings.
Street Food Culture
Kathmandu is famous for its diverse street food scene, offering a tantalizing array of local delicacies and flavors. From momos (dumplings) and sel roti (rice flour donuts) to chatpate (spicy snacks) and Newari specialties, the city's culinary delights are sure to delight food enthusiasts.
Warm Hospitality
One of Kathmandu's most endearing specialties is its warm and welcoming hospitality. Visitors are greeted with genuine warmth and friendliness wherever they go, making their stay in the city a truly memorable experience.

How to Reach Kathmandu

By Bike or Walking

Kathmandu is relatively compact and offers pleasant opportunities for walking and biking within the city. Many streets in the central areas are pedestrian-friendly, and there are designated bike lanes in some parts of the city.

Walking or biking is an excellent way to explore Kathmandu's vibrant neighborhoods, bustling markets, and historical sites. Most major attractions are within a manageable walking or biking distance from each other.

By Air

Tribhuvan International Airport (TIA) serves as the primary gateway to Kathmandu. Located about 6 kilometers east of the city center, TIA connects Kathmandu with major cities worldwide.
book now

Upon arrival at TIA, travelers can opt for various transportation options to reach Kathmandu city center, including:

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available outside the airport terminal. It's advisable to negotiate the fare before starting the journey.
  • Shuttle Service: Some hotels and guesthouses offer shuttle services to and from the airport. It's recommended to check with your accommodation beforehand if they provide this service.
  • Public Bus: Public buses run between the airport and Kathmandu city center. The bus stop is located outside the airport premises, across the parking area.
  • Rental Car: Several car rental agencies operate at TIA, allowing travelers to hire a vehicle for self-driving or with a driver.

It's essential to factor in potential traffic congestion, especially during peak hours, when planning your journey from the airport to Kathmandu.

highlight landmarks of Kathmandu

Boudhanath Stupa

One of the largest stupas in the world, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an important pilgrimage site for Buddhists.

Kathmandu Durbar Square

A historic square with numerous temples, palaces, and courtyards, showcasing traditional Newari architecture.

Swayambhunath Stupa

Also known as the Monkey Temple, it is one of the oldest and most sacred Buddhist sites in Nepal.

Kathmandu Valley

A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its rich culture, history, and stunning Himalayan landscapes.

Pashupatinath Temple

A significant Hindu temple complex located on the banks of the Bagmati River, dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Narayanhiti Palace Museum

Former royal palace turned museum, offering insights into Nepal's monarchy and history.

notable personalities of kathmandu

King Prithvi Narayan Shah

Known as the founder of modern Nepal, King Prithvi Narayan Shah was born in the Gorkha Kingdom, near Kathmandu. He unified several small kingdoms to establish the Kingdom of Nepal in the 18th century.

Anuradha Koirala

A prominent social activist and founder of Maiti Nepal, an organization dedicated to rescuing and rehabilitating victims of human trafficking. Anuradha Koirala's work has earned her international recognition, including CNN Hero of the Year in 2010.

Arniko (Anige)

A renowned architect, sculptor, and artist from the medieval period, Arniko was born in Kathmandu. He played a significant role in the spread of Nepalese art and architecture to Tibet and China during the 13th century.

Prabal Gurung

A globally acclaimed fashion designer, Prabal Gurung was born in Singapore but spent his formative years in Kathmandu. He has dressed numerous celebrities and political figures and is known for his innovative designs and advocacy for diversity in the fashion industry.

Bhaktapur Durbar Square Artisans

While not a single individual, the artisans of Bhaktapur Durbar Square in Kathmandu Valley deserve recognition for their exceptional craftsmanship. They have preserved traditional Newari art and architecture through centuries, creating intricate wood carvings, metalwork, and pottery.

Mahesh Chandra Regmi

A distinguished historian and scholar, Mahesh Chandra Regmi made significant contributions to the study of Nepalese history and culture. His works include numerous books and articles on various aspects of Nepalese society and governance.
